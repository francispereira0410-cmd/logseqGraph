summary:: Platform staff must check the platform–train interface and give assisted despatch when mirrors or monitors are defective. Train operators must check signals, doors and the platform, make announcements, and not move until authorised. Controllers and customer service supervisors must be told of incidents and coordinate detraining, last trains and overruns.

- Highlights first synced by [[Readwise]] [[Feb 27th, 2026]]
	- Category A platforms are those platforms where a train operator is unable to see the entire platform train interface from any part of the cab without the use of mirrors or monitors. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01k3nhrgy8m2gfjwnkhkx8p6wq))
	- Category B platforms are those platforms where a train operator can see the entire platform train interface by looking back from any part of the cab. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01k3nhsrrmw89z6r3wmem4bpp5))
	- You must not use the track retrieval device in the following circumstances: • platforms with overhead line equipment or within 2.75 metres of overhead line equipment • at platforms with platform edge doors • for large or heavy electronic tablets • in wet conditions. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01k3nj8ddz5k5ewh648qj7kt3q))
		- **Note**: 2.75m of wet peet.
	- You must only attempt to retrieve items from the area between the platform edge and the negative rail and only as far as you can reach, using a maximum of three pole extensions.
	  
	  You must not attempt to retrieve items from under the platform overhang or the area beyond the negative rail. You must not attempt to retrieve items which are in the pit. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01k3njbaz088gxxz2gd3gkq00a))
	- Controller Signaller ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01k3njs03ngwjapj8ypj68evws))
	- If protection is required, you must agree the appropriate method to be used with the signaller. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01k3njrvcvfwhz9nvk0z8kt0jq))
	- You must not move your train until authorised. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01k3njtw8v5nk10c3161gsj0ew))
	- When a train has overrun a platform, the controller will decide on the appropriate course of action to be taken depending on the circumstances.
	  
	  However, if it is likely that the incident will cause delays to the train service, the controller will normally authorise the train to continue to the next station without opening the doors. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01k3njvgre9p6e3wfnqmg4tqdp))
	- Assisted despatch is where platform staff are required to assist the train operator by checking part or all of the platform train interface on the train operator’s behalf.
	  
	  Platform staff will indicate they have done this using a signal which must be agreed with the train operator. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01k3nxdvg0nsma1a07wjxq1zfj))
	- Mirrors are required to be checked every 28 days. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01k3nxk85graj7f3b55yj30azf))
