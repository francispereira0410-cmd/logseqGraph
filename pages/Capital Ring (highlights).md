summary:: The Capital Ring walk from Wimbledon Park to Richmond is a seven-mile route with hills, nature, and historic sites like Wimbledon Windmill and Richmond Park. Along the way, you can enjoy pubs, cafes, toilets, and scenic views including Petersham Meadows and the Thames. The path ends near Richmond station, following the Thames Path and Richmond Bridge.

- Highlights first synced by [[Readwise]] [[Feb 27th, 2026]]
	- At seven miles this is one of the longer sections of the Capital Ring, with some quite long and steep ups and downs, mainly on rough tracks or paths and grass. There are steps on a footbridge over the A3. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kh65zdqeyrk3h0j19r1hb5zv))
	- The walk goes through Wimbledon Park, Wimbledon Common and past Wimbledon Windmill, before going through Richmond Park, London's largest national nature reserve. It also takes in Petersham Meadows, famous in the paintings of Turner. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kh5sd2faake72nc00mgp0znj))
